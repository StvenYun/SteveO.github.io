About Me

Hi! My name is Steven Yun, but my friends call me SteveO. I am a recent graduate of Stony Brook University with a degree in Mathematics and a double major in Applied Math. I am currently looking for opportunities that will challenge me on a daily basis.

With all of the free time acquired during the pandemic, I took it upon myself to be constantly learning new technologies that I could use to solve real world problems. I also happened to spend a lot of time in a Discord with my friends who are software engineers and fellow PC gamers. Alast, I came up with a great project idea; a discord bot for our discord server.

Initially, we were looking for a bot that would announce when somebody joined the voice call so we wouldn't have to alt-tab to the discord client. It seemed no such bot existed that was active at the time so I took it upon myself to start reading the discord.py docs and seeing what I could do with the library.

I started off small and worked my way up. The first bot I made that was useful in our server was a bot for our favorite PC game, League of Legends. The first command returned a link for an individual's op.gg link which returned stats of the player. It has since evolved to an ebedded message that contained all of that information without needing to return a link that sends you to another page!

I continued to look for other projects that I could make with this discord library. Since the beginning of the pandemic, I took interest in the stock market, and bio-tech stocks in specific. I spent days staring at my robinhood account portfolio to see many green and just as many red days. This gave me the idea of creating a fantasy stock trading bot which would allow us to look up prices/data, buy/sell, and pull up a portfolio. This would allow me and my friends to trade and talk about the market without the risk of losing money. I quickly got to work on devloping this. This was the first time I would be working with a database and I quickly came to realize how powerful an asset they can be. I had a friend help me map out the database and I went to work.

After finishing the stock bot, we finally came back around to our initial problem. We still wanted a bot that would announce when somebody joined the voice call. At this point I was very familiar with the discord.py library and I knew exactly what I needed to do to make this happen. These bots can be found in the discord bots section of my projects. This has sparked my interest in devloping and I hope to continue to learn and apply my knowledge in the world of development.
